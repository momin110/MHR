A brand new web developer in a company called Hot Beans would likely have several responsibilities and tasks to undertake. Onboarding and Orientation:

Familiarize themselves with the company culture, policies, and procedures. Get to know the team members and understand the organizational structure.

Training and Skill Development:

Engage in any necessary training programs to understand the company's development processes and tools. Acquire or enhance skills in relevant programming languages, frameworks, and technologies used by Hot Beans.

Project Understanding:

Review existing projects and understand the technologies and frameworks used by the company. Familiarize themselves with the development stack and tools employed in Hot Beans' projects.

Collaborate with other team members and departments to understand project requirements. Participate in team meetings and discussions to stay informed about ongoing projects.

Start working on coding tasks assigned by more senior developers or project managers. Contribute to the development of new features or enhancements for web applications.

Engage in code reviews to learn from more experienced developers and improve coding skills. Adapt to coding standards and best practices established within the company.

Learn to troubleshoot and debug issues in the codebase. Seek guidance from more experienced team members when encountering challenges.

Testing and Quality Assurance:

Assist in testing web applications to identify and fix bugs. Understand and contribute to maintaining code quality.

Documentation:

Document code changes, updates, and new features. Contribute to the development of internal documentation related to projects.

Continuous Learning:

Stay updated on industry trends, new technologies, and best practices in web development. Attend workshops, webinars, or training sessions to enhance skills.

Feedback and Improvement:

Seek feedback from colleagues and managers to identify areas for improvement. Proactively work towards enhancing skills and contributing more effectively to projects.

Adaptation to Company Processes:

Adapt to the company's project management and version control systems. Follow established workflows for code deployment and collaboration.
